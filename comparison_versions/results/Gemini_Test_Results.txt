Query:
"What should I do after a traffic accident?"

Answer:
After a traffic accident, the required steps depend on whether the accident resulted in injuries or only vehicle damage.

Steps for accidents with injuries:

Call traffic at 911 or 993.
Call the Red Crescent at 997.

Steps for accidents with damage:

Contact Najm and download the Najm application.
Take photographs of the accident from all angles.
Park the vehicle away from crowds, if possible.
Determine the location of the accident, enter the data, and confirm your phone number.
Submit a report containing the circumstances, location, type of accident, and types of vehicles involved.

Key Points:

Do not leave the scene without reason, as you will be considered a fugitive from justice and face legal action.
Only move the vehicle if it obstructs traffic, poses a threat to public safety, or if the accident is simple and both parties agree on how it happened.
Failure to comply with these laws can result in a fine of up to 2,000 Saudi riyals and/or imprisonment for up to 3 months.

Q2
--
Query:
"What should a driver do when approaching a roundabout?"

Answer:
When approaching a roundabout, a driver must plan their lane in advance and give way to vehicles already traveling inside the roundabout.

Steps:

Give way to vehicles that are already traveling around the roundabout.
Give a signal when navigating the roundabout.
Choose track 1 if you intend to turn right at the first exit.
Choose track 2, if available, if you intend to drive straight to the second exit.
Choose track 3, if available, if you intend to turn left at the third exit or make a full rotation at the fourth exit.

"What about priority?"

Answer:
Regarding roundabouts, priority is given to the vehicles already traveling inside it.

Key Points:

A driver approaching an intersection that forms a roundabout must give way to vehicles traveling around the roundabout.
Vehicles inside the roundabout have priority over vehicles outside it.
This priority rule applies in the absence of traffic lights or a traffic officer directing traffic.

Q4
--
Query:
"Can I let my friend drive my car without a license?"

Answer:
No, it is not allowed.

Key Points:

Allowing a person who does not hold a driving license to drive your vehicle is a traffic violation.
This rule applies to the vehicle's owner, designated driver, or possessor.
The penalty for this violation is a fine of not less than 1,000 riyals and not more than 2,000 riyals.
If allowing an unlicensed driver results in a traffic accident, both the owner and the driver shall be jointly liable, subject to the discretion of the competent court.

References:
Traffic Law.pdf, p.20, Article 77

Q6
--
Query:
"Is it allowed to use a phone while driving?"

Answer:
No, it is not allowed to use a mobile phone while driving without a hands-free device.

Key Points:

The rule applies to the driver of the vehicle.
Drivers must avoid distractions and must not drive while distracted by a mobile phone.
Using a mobile phone is a traffic violation unless a hands-free device is used.
Committing this violation results in 2 penalty points on the driver's record.

---
Query:
"Is modifying a car allowed?"

Answer:
Modifying a car is not allowed without prior permission.

Key Points:

The rule prohibits altering a vehicle's color, features, standard equipment, capacity, dimensions, weight, or engine power without authorization.
This applies to vehicle owners, repair shop owners, and workers.
Prior valid permission must be obtained from the competent authority before making any modifications.

Penalties:

Repair shop owners or workers who make unauthorized adjustments face a fine between 10,000 and 50,000 riyals.
The repair shop will also be closed for a period not exceeding three months.
Fines double and closure periods increase for repeat violations, leading to permanent closure for a third offense.

Query:
"Can the vehicles be moved before the accident is reported?"

Answer:
Yes, vehicles involved in an accident can be moved, but only under specific circumstances.

Key Points:

Vehicles may be moved if they obstruct traffic or pose a threat to public safety.
Vehicles may be moved if the accident was simple and clear, and both parties agreed on how it happened.
You should take photographs of the accident from all angles (such as through the Najm application) before parking the vehicle away from crowds.
